Core Focus, Approaches, and Goal

I plan to establish an interdisciplinary research group that serves as a data-to-insight engine for interrogating tumor ecosystems through mapping, modeling, and modulation.

  • Mapping cellular architecture using spatial, single-cell, and other genomics technologies to define how malignant, immune, and stromal states are organized in space and time.
  • Modelling molecular, cellular, and clinical variables using machine learning, deep learning, digital pathology, and large language models to uncover the principles that govern ecosystem dynamics and therapeutic response.
  • Modulating tumor states through experimental and therapeutic perturbations to identify actionable vulnerabilities and develop strategies that improve cancer immunotherapy.

Future Directions

My future laboratory will collaborate closely with clinical, experimental, and computational partners to drive three interconnected research directions that advance precision cancer immunotherapy.

Direction 1

Spatiotemporal Mapping of Tumor Ecosystems

Using single-cell, spatial, and multi-omics technologies, we will chart how tumor and microenvironmental cells co-evolve across disease progression and therapeutic contexts. By integrating cutting-edge genomics with bioinformatics and machine learning, we will identify coordinated cell-state modules, delineate spatial niches, and map tumor–immune co-evolution. These efforts will uncover the organizational principles of tumor ecosystems and generate actionable biomarkers to inform treatment strategies.

Direction 2

AI-Powered Virtual Tumor Models

We will develop AI-powered, ecosystem-informed virtual tumor models that integrate genomic, single-cell, spatial, imaging, and clinical data to guide personalized therapy selection. By combining variational autoencoders, graph neural networks, and multimodal transformers, we will construct patient-specific digital twins capable of predicting therapeutic responses and disease trajectories. These models will serve as computational testbeds for designing rational and personalized treatment strategies.

Direction 3

Immunotherapy Optimization and Innovation

Building on these maps and models, we will identify molecular and cellular predictors of response, resistance, and toxicity. Integrative analyses will reveal context-dependent cell states and signaling networks that govern immunotherapy outcomes. Through iterative in silico perturbations and experimental validation, we will develop a prediction–validation–retraining framework to refine therapeutic strategies and accelerate translational discovery.

Summary and Vision

From Mapping to Modeling to Modulation

Together, these three research directions form an integrated, data-driven pipeline—from mapping to modeling to modulation—that bridges the gap between biological discovery and clinical translation. The long-term vision of my lab is to establish a genomics- and AI-powered translational oncology platform that transforms how we understand, predict, and treat cancer.

Through this interdisciplinary approach, we aim to build the foundation for ecosystem-informed precision immunotherapy, where the complexity of tumor biology is harnessed—not hindered—to guide the next generation of cancer treatments.
